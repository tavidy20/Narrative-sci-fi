Types of Narrative Text

A narrative is a story, and stories can be categorized into several main types based on their content and purpose. Here are some of the most common examples:

  • 1. Fables
    These are short stories where the main characters are usually animals that talk and act like humans. Their purpose is to teach a moral lesson. Example: The Tortoise and the Hare, which teaches that consistency is more important than speed.
  • 2. Fairy Tales
    Fairy tales are stories that often involve magic, mythical creatures (like fairies, dragons, or giants), and a fantasy world. They often have a happy ending. Example: Cinderella, Snow White.
  • 3. Legends
    A legend is a folk tale that is believed to have a historical basis and often explains the origin of a place, a natural phenomenon, or an object. Example: The Legend of King Arthur.
  • 4. Myths
    Myths are ancient stories that aim to explain the origin of the universe, gods and goddesses, or natural events. They are considered sacred by a particular culture. Example: Greek Myths of Zeus and Olympus.
  • 5. Folktales
    These are stories passed down orally from generation to generation, reflecting the values and traditions of a culture. They can be humorous, adventurous, or even scary. Example: Bawang Merah & Bawang Putih, The Boy Who Cried Wolf.
  • 6. Horror Stories
    A narrative created to evoke feelings of fear, suspense, and dread in the reader. These stories often involve ghosts, monsters, or psychological threats. Example: Dracula, urban legends.
  • 7. Fantasy
    A fictional story set in a non-real world that often involves magic, magical creatures (elves, orcs), and a battle between good and evil. Example: Harry Potter, The Lord of the Rings.
  • 8. Sci-Fi (Science Fiction)
    This genre uses speculative science and technology as the main elements of the story. These narratives often explore the future, robots, space travel, or the effects of technology on humanity. Example: Star Wars, The Matrix.
  • 9. Mystery
    A story centered on solving a puzzle, a crime, or a strange event. The hero of the story is often a detective who looks for clues to uncover the truth. Example: Sherlock Holmes.
  • 10. Life Stories (Biography/Autobiography)
    These are non-fiction narratives about a person's life. A biography is written by someone else, while an autobiography is written by the person themselves. Example: Nelson Mandela’s biography.
