Direct & Indirect (Reported) Speech

Clear rules, tables, lots of sci-fi examples, and a tiny interactive demo to practise converting Direct → Indirect.

1. What they are

Direct speech reports someone's exact words and is written inside quotation marks: He said, "I am reading."

Indirect (reported) speech paraphrases the words without quotation marks and often shifts tense, pronouns, and time expressions: He said (that) he was reading.

2. Main changes when you transform Direct → Indirect

a) Tense changes (typical)

DirectIndirect
"I am reading."He said (that) he was reading.
"I read the book."She said (that) she had read the book.
"I will go tomorrow."He said (that) he would go the next day.
"I have finished."They said (that) they had finished.

If the reporting verb is in present or future (e.g., says, will say), tense shift is optional or not required.

b) Pronoun & perspective

Pronouns change to match the new speaker/listener perspective.

  • "She said, 'I will help you.'" → She said that she would help me.
  • If the speaker stays the same in context, sometimes pronouns remain—consider who "I"/"you" refer to.

c) Time/place expressions

DirectIndirect
todaythat day
tomorrowthe next day / the following day
yesterdaythe day before / the previous day
nowthen
last nightthe night before

3. Reporting verbs (common patterns)

  • said / told — for statements. (She said that... / He told me that...)
  • asked / wanted to know — for questions. (He asked if/whether... / She wanted to know when...)
  • ordered / told / advised / suggested — for commands & suggestions (use to + infinitive).

4. Forms: statements, questions, commands

a) Statements

Direct: She said, "I love sci-fi movies."
Indirect: She said (that) she loved sci-fi movies.

b) Yes/No questions

Use if or whether:

Direct: He asked, "Do you like Interstellar?"
Indirect: He asked if I liked Interstellar.

c) Wh-questions

Kata tanya tetap digunakan — tanpa tanda tanya pada akhir:

Direct: She asked, "Where are the astronauts now?"
Indirect: She asked where the astronauts were.

d) Commands / Requests

Gunakan to + infinitive setelah reporting verb:

Direct: The captain said, "Open the spaceship door!"
Indirect: The captain told them to open the spaceship door.

5. Sci-fi contextual examples (short)

Direct: The robot said, "I can detect alien life forms."
Indirect: The robot said that it could detect alien life forms.
Direct: The astronaut asked, "When will the spaceship land?"
Indirect: The astronaut asked when the spaceship would land.
Direct: The scientist said, "We discovered a new galaxy yesterday."
Indirect: The scientist said that they had discovered a new galaxy the day before.

6. Handy quick-reference summary

  • Tense: usually shift back (present → past, past → past perfect, will → would).
  • Pronouns: change to match new speaker/listener.
  • Time/Place words: adjust (today → that day, tomorrow → the next day).
  • Questions: yes/no → use if/whether; wh-questions → keep wh-word.
  • Commands: use to + infinitive with verbs like told, ordered.

8. Tips for students

  • First find the reporting verb (said/asked/told) and its tense.
  • Identify the speaker & listener to fix pronouns.
  • Adjust tense and any time words (today → that day, etc.).
  • For questions, keep the question word but remove the question mark in indirect form.
  • When in doubt, rewrite the sentence in natural English — clarity beats literal swapping.
