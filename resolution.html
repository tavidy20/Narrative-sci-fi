The Resolution is the part of a narrative text where the conflict that drives the story is finally solved. This section provides closure to the plot and shows how the characters deal with the challenges they have faced. Depending on the story, a resolution can bring joy, sadness, or even leave the audience questioning what happens next.

Types of Resolution

  • Happy Ending: The problem ends in a positive way. The characters achieve their goals or find peace. Example: Cinderella marries the Prince and they live happily ever after.
  • Sad Ending: The story concludes tragically. The characters may fail or lose something important. Example: Romeo and Juliet die in the end.
  • Open Ending: The ending is left uncertain. The readers must imagine what happens next. Example: Inception, where the spinning top leaves the audience wondering.
