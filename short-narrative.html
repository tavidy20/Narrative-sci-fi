Tips for Writing a Short Sci-Fi Narrative

  • Orientation: Introduce setting, time, place, and main characters.
  • Complication: Present a conflict, mysterious event, or challenge.
  • Resolution: Show how the problem is solved or conclude with a suspenseful ending.
  • Use vivid and descriptive language to create imagination and interest.
  • Keep logical flow: each element should connect naturally to the next.
  • Check spelling, grammar, and clarity.

Example Short Sci-Fi Story

Orientation: Year 2425, aboard the starship Horizon. Captain Elara, Science Officer Mira, and Lieutenant Kael are preparing to investigate energy readings on the uncharted planet Xythera.

Complication: Strange anomalies distort the ship's instruments and the crew discovers a mysterious alien structure that seems to shift dimensions.

Resolution: Using advanced sensors and teamwork, the crew deciphers the structure's patterns, stabilizes the anomaly, and safely documents their findings.
