The Re-orientation (also called Coda) is the final part of a narrative text that provides closure, reflection, or a moral lesson. Unlike the resolution, which focuses on solving the conflict, re-orientation emphasizes what readers can learn from the story. This part is optional, but it often appears in traditional tales, fables, and stories with moral values.

Purpose:

  • To highlight the lesson learned from the characters‚Äô experiences.
  • To provide reflection or advice for readers.
  • To make the story meaningful beyond just entertainment.

Nature:

Re-orientation is not always included in every narrative text, especially in modern stories. However, when present, it gives deeper meaning and leaves a lasting impression on the audience.
