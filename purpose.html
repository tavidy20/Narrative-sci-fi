The Purpose of Narrative Text

The main purpose of a narrative text is to entertain the reader or listener. Beyond this primary goal, a narrative text also serves several other important purposes:

  • To Teach a Moral Lesson: Many stories, such as fables and fairy tales, are created to teach lessons about right and wrong. For example, the story of The Tortoise and the Hare teaches the value of persistence, while a story like Malin Kundang teaches about the consequences of disrespect.
  • To Entertain: This is the most basic purpose. Narrative texts are designed to make readers feel happy, tense, sad, or surprised through an engaging plot and interesting characters. Novels, movies, and comics are examples of media that focus heavily on this purpose.
  • To Inspire: Stories about struggle, success, or resilience often inspire readers to be better in their own lives. Biographies or tales of heroes frequently have this purpose.
  • To Inform: While not the main goal, some narratives can serve as a way to pass on historical or cultural information. Legends and folktales are often used to preserve knowledge and beliefs from one generation to the next.
  • To Evoke Emotion: Narrative texts have the power to create various emotions in the reader, such as fear in a horror story, warmth in a romance, or curiosity in a mystery.

In short, the primary purpose of a narrative text is to entertain, but it often contains a deeper message or lesson that the author wants to convey.
