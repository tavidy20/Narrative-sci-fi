Orientation

In a narrative text, the Orientation is the very first stage of the story. Its function is to introduce the audience to the world of the story by giving them enough background information to understand what is happening. Without Orientation, readers or viewers may feel lost because they do not know who the characters are, where the story is set, or when the events are taking place.

Orientation usually covers three important elements:

Key Elements:

  • Who: The introduction of the main characters. This may include the protagonist (main hero), the antagonist (enemy or conflict creator), and supporting characters. A clear introduction helps the audience build a connection with the characters.
  • Where: The setting of the story. Is it in a real place, such as a city, a forest, or outer space? Or is it in an imaginary or fantasy world?
  • When: The time when the story happens. Is it in the past, present, future, or in a timeless, magical period?
