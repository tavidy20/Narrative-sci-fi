Complication

In a narrative text, the Complication is the core part of the story where the conflict begins to appear and develop. Without complication, a story would be flat and uninteresting because there would be no problems to solve. Complication introduces challenges, struggles, and obstacles that the characters must face. It is the part that makes readers or viewers curious about how the story will continue.

Sources of Conflict:

  • Man vs. Man: Conflict between two characters, such as a hero against a villain or two friends in disagreement.
  • Man vs. Nature: Conflict between a character and natural forces like storms, disasters, or harsh environments.
  • Man vs. Himself: Internal conflict within the character, such as fear, doubt, guilt, or difficult decisions.
  • Man vs. Society: Conflict between the character and societal rules, norms, or traditions that limit their freedom.

Climax

The complication usually builds up towards the Climax, which is the highest point of tension in the story. At this stage, the character must face the problem directly, and the outcome will determine how the story proceeds.
