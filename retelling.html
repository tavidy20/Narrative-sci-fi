What is Retelling?

Retelling is the ability to recount a story or text using your own words while keeping the main ideas, sequence, and important details. This practice is essential in speaking because it improves comprehension, vocabulary use, fluency, and confidence. In this module, we will focus on retelling science fiction (sci-fi) stories.

Steps in Retelling a Story

  1. Read or Listen to the story carefully. Pay attention to the beginning, middle, and ending.
  2. Identify the Main Characters (heroes, villains, or creatures in sci-fi).
  3. Note the Setting (place and time â€“ e.g., another planet, the future, or a spaceship).
  4. Find the Problem or conflict faced by the characters.
  5. Observe the Solution or how the characters overcome the problem.
  6. Retell the Story in your own words. Do not memorize; instead, reconstruct the story logically.

Example: Short Sci-Fi Story

Original text:

"In the year 2145, Earth was no longer safe. Humanity built giant ships to travel across galaxies. One young pilot, Aira, discovered a hidden planet with strange glowing forests. But the planet was guarded by machines that tested her bravery. In the end, Aira proved her courage, and the machines welcomed humans to settle there."

Retelling in simple words:

"The story is about the future when Earth was unsafe. People traveled to other galaxies. A young pilot named Aira found a planet with glowing forests. The planet had machines that tested her, but she showed bravery and gained permission for humans to live there."

Practice Activity

Now try to retell the story step by step:

  • Who is the main character?
  • Where and when does the story happen?
  • What problem does the character face?
  • How is the problem solved?
  • Retell the story in your own words (2-3 sentences, then expand to 5-6 sentences).

Tips for Good Retelling

  • Use your own words, do not memorize exactly.
  • Speak with clear pronunciation and proper pauses.
  • Include connectors like first, then, after that, finally.
  • Use intonation to show emotions (fear, excitement, courage).
  • Practice with both short retelling (summary) and long retelling (detailed version).

Extra Practice Stories

Story 1: The Robot Friend

"Lena lived on a space station orbiting Mars. She felt lonely until she built a small robot named Zino. One day, the station lost power, and Zino risked itself to restore the energy. Thanks to the robot, Lena and the crew survived."

Story 2: The Time Door

"In the ruins of an old city, a boy named Kai discovered a glowing door. When he stepped through, he entered the year 3020. He saw flying cities and talking animals, but the door began to close. He had to decide whether to stay in the future or return home."

Story 3: The Alien Message

"Scientists received a signal from deep space. It was a map leading to a hidden moon. When astronauts arrived, they found giant crystals glowing with strange energy. The crystals were actually a message: 'We are not enemies. We wait for peace.'"
