Write the Orientation

This module guides you on how to write the Orientation section of a Sci-Fi story. You will learn its essential elements, strategies, and see examples before writing your own text.

Instructions:

  1. Read the tips and examples carefully.
  2. Focus on the key elements: setting, time, place, characters, and initial situation.
  3. Take notes or draft ideas before writing your orientation.
  4. Write at least 5–7 sentences that clearly introduce your story.
  5. Use your imagination while keeping logical flow in the story.
  6. Click Play Audio to hear the example narration.
  7. After writing, click Submit to receive feedback.

Tips for Writing a Strong Orientation

  • Setting: Describe the time (future, year, era) and place (planet, spaceship, city) to create a vivid context.
  • Characters: Introduce main characters with names and roles.
  • Initial Situation: Mention the problem, mission, or event that starts the story.
  • Atmosphere: Include mood or tone (mysterious, exciting, tense).
  • Engaging Hook: Start with an interesting sentence to capture attention.
  • Clarity and Cohesion: Make sure sentences are logical and easy to understand.

Example Orientation

Setting: Year 2425, aboard the starship Horizon, orbiting the uncharted planet Xythera.

Characters: Captain Elara, Science Officer Mira, Lieutenant Kael.

Initial Situation: The crew prepares to investigate mysterious energy readings, unaware of the anomalies awaiting them.
